For most of the last decade, renewable investment dollars went overwhelmingly toward generation — solar panels and wind turbines. That balance is changing.

## Storage catches up

Battery storage investment has grown faster than generation investment for three straight years, as grid operators grapple with the intermittency of solar and wind at higher penetration levels.

## What's driving it

Falling battery cell costs, new long-duration storage chemistries, and utility procurement rules that increasingly require paired storage are all contributing. Analysts expect storage to represent close to a third of total clean energy investment within the next few years.
