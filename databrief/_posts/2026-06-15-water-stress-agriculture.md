Water-intensive crops are migrating away from historically productive but increasingly water-stressed regions, toward areas with more reliable rainfall or irrigation access.

## The migration in numbers

Several major agricultural basins have seen measurable declines in groundwater-dependent crop acreage over the past five years, replaced by less water-intensive alternatives or entirely different land use.

## The long-term risk

Even regions currently considered water-secure are trending toward higher stress levels within a generation, according to most hydrological models, meaning today's migration pattern is likely an early chapter rather than a resolution.
