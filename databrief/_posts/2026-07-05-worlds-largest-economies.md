Global GDP rankings shift slowly, but the gaps between them are narrowing. Emerging economies are closing ground on established ones faster than at any point in the past two decades.

## The current order

The top five economies still account for the majority of global output, but their combined share has been gradually declining as growth in South and Southeast Asia outpaces the historical leaders.

## Why the ranking is converging

Population growth, manufacturing relocation, and rising domestic consumption are the three biggest drivers behind the narrowing gap. Meanwhile, several mature economies are growing at rates closer to 1–2% annually, compared to 5%+ in the fastest-growing large economies.
